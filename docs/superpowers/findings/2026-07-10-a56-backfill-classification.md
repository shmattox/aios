# A56 backfill classification — reconciled

Five parallel classifiers (`pbatch_00`, `pbatch_01`, `pbatch_02`, `gbatch_00`, `gbatch_01`) each ran a first pass over the 217 retired wiki source-stubs from H39's FO drain, using inconsistent bars — one (`pbatch_01`) explicitly cross-checked every candidate against existing `knowledge/` pages before calling anything concept-rich; the other four did not. This pass re-judges every "concept-rich" claim from all five batches against one uniform, skeptical bar (transferable idea + enough substance in the stub + **not already covered** by an existing knowledge page) and reads the actual target pages to verify, rather than trusting the batch's self-report.

## Headline

Of 217 retired stubs: **9 genuine gaps** (collapsing to ~7 target pages, 2 of the 9 marginal), **75 already-covered** (the insight was already distilled or explicitly cited on a live knowledge page — the classifiers just hadn't checked), **42 concept-thin** (title/teaser only, nothing to synthesize), **89 correctly-retired reference**. The dominant failure mode in the four undisciplined batches wasn't missed synthesis — it was **not checking `knowledge/` before calling something a loss**. `pbatch_02`'s 20 "concept-rich" calls collapsed to 6 genuine + 14 already-covered on inspection (Satya Nadella and the "Single Company Brain" post are both already fully distilled with the exact same facts in `agent-harness.md`; the entire holodeck/projection-mapping cluster is already scoped in `holodeck-immersive-projection.md`, which explicitly declares itself technically complete). `gbatch_00`'s 17 "concept-rich" calls collapsed to **zero** genuine gaps — every single one is cited by exact raw path in `claude-code-ecosystem-adoption.md`'s "Source pages" section, which is a comprehensive citation index for exactly that 2026-05-29 starred-repo batch. This says enhanced-distill depth is **not** worth wiring live as a standing mechanism: the real recovered-value pile is ~9 stubs worth of content, most of it foldable into existing pages as subsections rather than new pages, and the actual lesson is a **dedup-before-flagging discipline gate**, not a deeper distillation pass.

## Concept-thin (Leg-1 evidence, not backfill) — 42

Titles/teasers with zero rendered body content — the actual technique lives at an unfetched source (a thread whose body never rendered, a video with no transcript, an X post that's media-only). This is not a backfill failure; it's a **capture-depth** failure at ingest time. No amount of re-reading the stub recovers the content because the content was never captured. The fix, if any, is deeper capture (fetch full thread/transcript text before landing the stub, not just the headline), not a richer distill pass over what's already on disk. ## Recommendation

**Do not wire enhanced-distill depth as a standing mechanism.** The four undisciplined batches' inflated concept-rich counts (4+3+20+17+1 = 45 raw claims) were driven almost entirely by skipping the "does `knowledge/` already say this?" check — once applied, 34 of `pbatch_00`/`pbatch_02`'s claims and all 17 of `gbatch_00`'s collapsed to already-covered. The one batch that ran the dedup check itself (`pbatch_01`) needed zero correction. **The actual lesson from A56 is process, not engine depth: any future backfill/distill pass must open the proposed target page and grep for the raw path before calling something a gap — that single step would have cut this exercise's manual-verification workload by ~80%.** The real recovered-value pile is small and mostly cheap: ~7 solid items, five of which are single subsections on two already-existing pages (`agent-harness.md`, `loop-engineering.md`) rather than new pages — an hour or two of drafting, not a new pipeline capability. Worth doing by hand next session; not worth building a mechanism for.
